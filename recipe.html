A Rice How To

(Cooking for people who don't want to cook)

Why rice?

  • Healthy - low in sodium and a great source of energy
  • Cheap - save those $£¥
  • Fairly easy to make (Once you get the hang of it!)
  • Flexible - tastes great with lots of different foods

What you'll need

  • Rice
  • Pan with Lid & Stove (duh)
  • Water
  • Cup for measuring

Use the cup to measure the rice and the water ...

Directions

  1. Measure out some rice - I use a small glass and make about 250ml worth. Don't worry if you make too much at first, you'll know better for next time.
  2. Pour the rice in the pan and then add the same amount (plus a little bit more) of water to the pan.
  3. Cover the lid of the pan and put it on a burner on the stove on a high temperature.
  4. When the water's boiling (it should be fairly obvious), remove the pan from the heat and allow it to cool for a few moments. It should no longer be bubbling.
  5. Lower the temperature of the burner (I use the minimum setting) and put the pan back on.
  6. Wait about 12 minutes ...
  7. Your rice should be ready to eat!

Not super easy to tell in this picture, but it should be noticeable that the rice has soaked up all of the water ...

And add a bit of chicken and voilà, dinner is served!
